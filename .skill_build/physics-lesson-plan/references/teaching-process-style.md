# Teaching Process Style

Use this reference when drafting the final `教与学活动` cells.

## Core Standard

The final teaching-process body must read like a concrete classroom implementation record, not like a question-answer outline.

Keep anchor labels such as `活动一：...`, `核心问题1：...`, `递进型问题1-1-1：...`, or the user-requested `问题1：...` so the teacher can check coverage. These labels are only anchors. The main body after each anchor must be natural teaching language.

## Required Texture

A strong classroom paragraph normally includes:

- Teacher setup: what the teacher displays, demonstrates, distributes, writes, asks, or organizes.
- Student action: what students observe, discuss, operate, record, draw, calculate, compare, report, or revise.
- Dialogue: use `师：` and `生：`, but do not reduce the whole paragraph to one teacher question and one student answer.
- Teacher follow-up: include `追问`, `补充`, `点拨`, `引导`, or `提示` when students answer incompletely or show a misconception.
- Teacher circulation: include `教师巡视` in experiment, drawing, discussion, or group-work stages.
- Feedback and evidence: state what the teacher checks, what counts as evidence, how errors are corrected, and what conclusion students form.
- Transition: show how this moment leads naturally to the next question, activity, concept, calculation, or transfer task.

## DO NOT

- DO NOT write only `师：问题。生：答案。` repeated line by line. Reason: this is a question-answer script, not a teachable lesson process.
- DO NOT place all confirmed questions first and all activities later. Reason: each question must drive the next student action or teacher guidance.
- DO NOT write a bare activity label without describing how students actually learn inside it. Reason: labels cannot replace classroom implementation.
- DO NOT use rigid checklist labels such as `教师提出`, `学生预设`, `教师追问`, `评价反馈` as visible headings. Reason: these are internal checks; the final text should be smooth and natural.

## Preferred Pattern

For each major activity, write in this rhythm:

1. Name the activity and core question.
2. Let the teacher create a situation or task.
3. Insert one or two anchor questions at the point where they actually occur.
4. Write likely student responses, including one possible incomplete answer or misconception when useful.
5. Write teacher follow-up, operation guidance,巡视 feedback, or board organization.
6. Let students produce evidence: data, drawing, explanation, comparison, conclusion, or report.
7. Use a transition sentence to enter the next activity.

## Example Skeleton

```text
活动二：探究重力与质量的关系
**核心问题2：重力的大小与质量有什么关系？怎样用实验证据说明？**
教师出示弹簧测力计和不同质量的钩码，先请学生用手掂一掂并说出直观感受。
递进型问题2-1-1：质量不同的物体给手臂的感觉有什么不同？这种感觉能直接作为科学结论吗？
生：质量大的钩码更“沉”，但只是感觉，还需要测量。
师：对，感觉可以提出猜想，但结论必须来自证据。要把猜想变成证据，我们需要测量哪两个量？
探究型问题2-2-1：本实验要改变什么、测量什么、记录什么？
学生小组讨论后选择器材，说明弹簧测力计用于测重力，钩码用于改变质量，表格用于记录数据。教师巡视各组，提醒学生先检查测力计是否调零，读数时视线要与刻度线相平，钩码静止后再记录。
汇报时，教师选取一组规范数据和一组存在偏差的数据并列呈现，追问：同样质量下数据为什么会不同？学生从调零、读数角度分析误差。教师据此反馈操作规范，再引导学生比较 `G/m` 的比值，归纳重力与质量成正比，并自然过渡到公式 `G=mg`。
```
